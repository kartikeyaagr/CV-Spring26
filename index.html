Why does this happen?

1. Face Distortion

When I take a picture close up, the subject's nose looks abnormally large because it's so much closer to the camera than their ears. By stepping back and zooming in, I increase the overall distance, so the slight difference between the nose and ears becomes negligible. This "flattens" the face and makes it look more natural.

2. Street Compression

For the street scene, the first photo (zoomed in from far away) looks compressed. Because I'm standing so far away, the distant objects don't shrink as much as we expect them to. In the second photo (close up without zoom), the depth is exaggerated, making the street look much longer and the background much smaller.

3. The Main Idea (Projections)

The goal is to compare Perspective Projection (how we normally see things, where objects get smaller the further they are) with Orthographic Projection (where size stays the same regardless of distance). By using a high zoom from a distance, we mimic an orthographic view by minimizing the effect of perspective.
